\# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) \& Business Intelligence



\## Dataset



For this project, I used an "Ecommerce Sales dataset" that contains details like order date, product name, category, region, quantity, sales, and profit. This dataset helped me understand how an online business performs.



\---



\## Tools Used



* &#x20;Microsoft Excel – for basic analysis and creating charts
* &#x20;PostgreSQL – for writing queries and answering business questions



\---



\##  Business Questions



While working on this dataset, I tried to answer the following questions:



1\. Which are the top 5 products generating the highest sales?

2\. How do sales change over time (monthly trend)?

3\. Which category contributes the most to total revenue?

4\. Which region performs the best in terms of sales?

5\. What is the average order value?

6\. Which products are sold the most (based on quantity)?

7\. In which month are sales the highest?



\---



\##  Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)



I started by exploring the dataset in Excel.



&#x20;Checked the data and understood each column

&#x20;Calculated basic values like average, maximum, and minimum sales

&#x20;Created charts to visualize the data:



&#x20;  A histogram to understand sales distribution

&#x20;  A bar chart to compare category-wise sales

&#x20;  A line chart to see monthly sales trends



This helped me get a clear overview of how the business is performing.



\---



\## 🧠 Insights



* After analyzing the data, I found some interesting patterns:



* &#x20;A few products are responsible for a large portion of total sales, which shows strong customer demand for those items.



* &#x20;Sales are not constant throughout the year. Some months perform better than others, which indicates possible seasonal trends.



* &#x20;One category clearly stands out in terms of revenue, making it the most important for the business.



* &#x20;Sales differ across regions, with certain regions contributing more than others. This shows that location plays a role in customer demand.



* &#x20;Some products are sold in much higher quantities compared to others, showing customer preference.



* &#x20;The average order value gives an idea of how much customers typically spend per purchase.



* &#x20;There are specific months where sales peak, which can help businesses plan offers, marketing, and stock in advance.
